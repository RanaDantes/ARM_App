What is ARM?


Association Rule Mining (ARM) is a data mining technique used to uncover hidden patterns or relationships between variables in large datasets, especially in transaction data. It helps identify powerful if-then associations‚Äîalso called association rules‚Äîthat reveal how items co-occur in real-world data.

|    If a customer buys item A, how likely will he/she also buys item B?

ARM is best known for market basket analysis, where businesses discover which items are often purchased together. The process involves two main steps:


  1. Frequent Itemset Generation ‚Äî Identifying item combinations that appear often above a support threshold.
  2. Rule Generation ‚Äî Forming association rules based on metrics like support, confidence, and lift.

A key algorithm in ARM is Apriori, which smartly reduces the search space using the principle that all subsets of a frequent itemset must also be frequent. By converting raw data into meaningful rules, ARM enables data-driven decision-making in business and research.
