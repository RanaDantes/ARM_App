What is ARM?

Associate Rule Mining (ARM) is a data mining technique used to uncover hidden patterns, correlations, and relationships within large datasets. Its primary goal is to discover interesting if-then relationships, known as association rules, that help describe how items or attributes co-occur in data.

Originally popularized through market basket analysis, ARM helps answer questions like:

|    If a customer buys item A, how likely are they to also buy item B?
The ARM process typically involves two keys steps:
1. Frequent Itemset Generation - Identifyng item combinations that occur together frequently above a minimum support threshold.
2. Rule Generation - Creating strong and valid rules from these itemsets based on metrics like support, confidece, and lift.

One of the most foundational algorithms used in ARM is the Apriori algorithm, which systematically reduces the search space using the principle that all subsets of a frequent itemset must also be frequent. By translating raw data into understandable rules, ARM enables organizations to extract actionable insights and enhance strategic outcomes.
